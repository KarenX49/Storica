Ancient History

Wine has been produced in the land where Armenia is currently located for over 6,000 years, confirmed by a team of researchers in 2007, from UCLA and the Armenian Institute of Archaeology and Ethnography, who discovered the oldest winemaking facility in the world, the Areni-1 cave, located in Areni, Vayots Dzor.

From it’s ancient roots, through a transitional era of privatization due to the dissolution of the USSR, and during modern times, wine production and grape- growing has survived much like the Armenian people themselves, across centuries and millennia.
